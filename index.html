About

I graduated from the Magistère de Physique Fondamentale d'Orsay (Master's degree in Fundamental Physics) and am certified to teach Physics and Chemistry (with a focus on Physics). I completed my education with a Master's degree in Astrophysics. I am currently working on my thesis at LIRA, where my research focuses on the development of direct imaging of exoplanets for the coronagraphic instrument of the future Roman Space Telescope.

The objective of my thesis is to study and optimize innovative techniques such as active correction (dark hole) or post-processing correction (coherence differential imaging) applied to the Roman Space Telescope coronagraph through numerical simulations and laboratory tests on the THD2 test bench. This telescope will serve as a technology demonstrator for the future Habitable Worlds Observatory mission.

Research

I began my adventure at LIRA with my master's internship, studying Coherence Differential Imaging (CDI) for direct imaging of exoplanets. The goal of the internship was to implement and study the CDI technique using codes developed at LIRA, in order to optimize it on data from numerical simulations of the VLT/SPHERE instrument and a state-of-the-art optical bench: the THD2. This work aims to pave the way for a novel and innovative strategy to be deployed for the next survey of giant exoplanets by direct imaging with SPHERE+.

I have decided to pursue a PhD between 2025 and 2028 with Johan Mazoyer, Axel Potier, and Pierre Baudoz for direct imaging with the Roman Space Telescope. The goal is to study and optimize innovative techniques such as active correction (dark hole) or post-processing correction (CDI) applied to the Roman Space Telescope's coronagraph through numerical simulations and laboratory testing on the THD2 bench. I work closely with several teams around the world. This telescope will serve as a technology demonstrator for the future Habitable Worlds Observatory mission, which aims to image planets similar to our own Earth.
